Denver Public Schools Routing

In this project, I helped the DPS School district route vehicles that were used for delivering lunches to students. DPS has a system where instead of every school having a cafeteria, some schools do and produce extra meals which are delivered to those who don't.

This was a multi stage project where I worked with a team, and I was responsible for the routing solution. This problem is infamously known as the Vehicle Routing Problem or VRP for short. It's an NP-Hard problem which is similar to putting together several Travelling Salesman Problems together.

Without an industrial grade solver, I knew getting an optimal solution was out of the picture. So, to come up with a feasible solution, I adapted an existing algorithm - the Clarke-Wright Savings Algorithm. To address the specific constraints of this problem, though, I read through academic papers and created some of my own solutions to make sure generated solutions are entirely feasible. After a lot of mistakes, I was able to produce a solution which proposed a routing scheme that allowed DPS to reduce their current 11 routes to 9.
